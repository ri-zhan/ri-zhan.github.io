Invisible Man

A design project, currently being coded to real life.

Challenge

The challenge was to redesign a digital library in 48 hours, where users can borrow and return books. By improving access to books and literature, it reflects TCLI Foundation’s goal towards effecting societal changes through literature, arts and culture.

Solution

Our application provides users with a wide selection of literature with common word choices in order to be more accessible to a wider range of audiences. We focused on user engagement through gamification and engaging the users in a community, as well as user-friendliness through simple word choices and information buttons.

Opening Sequence

the final project considers usability issues, by incorporating instructions at first, it clarifies the direction which the user is supposed to take

Surprise Sequence

This website features a surprise sequence, a reflection of the plotline which is mysterious and curious. The transition is very subtle, where the bottom caption changes from #/6 to ?/6, which indicates the beginning of the Surprise Sequence.

Edge cases

In the case that the user does not know what to do, the card will pop out using a red outline and a slight movement. Non-major cards will also get a black overlay to further emphasize the main component.

Process

Ideation

The first phase was ideation, where I thought of creating a book, a card game, or i-spy game.

Lo-fi

This part started by sketching out the images in the each card and quickly prototyping the positions of the cards and how they would interact with the users.

Hi-fi

In the hi-fi stage, I converted the card images to SVG files and refined the prototyping, such as the transition periods and animations.

Critiques

Since this is a school project, we had weekly class sessions, in which she would check-in with out progress and provide feedback.

User Testing

In the user testing stage (before the final project was due), I asked a couple of classmates to try out the game and ask about their experience, if there were anything missing, or if something could be improved.
