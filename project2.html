about

Challenge

The challenge for this project was to communicate a film or movie in a web-based digital experience. We were restricted from using images directly from the story we chose to avoid replicating the motions of the film or movie.

Solution

I chose to do this project on "The Invisible Man" by H. G. Wells. I chose to gamify the experience as an I-Spy game, with 6 cards rotating inspired by the motions of Russian Roulette.

My Role

Ideation, User Experience, User Testing, Interaction Design

Timeline

2 month, final project for DESN 1011 Visual Interaction Design at York University

Tools

Figma, Procreate, Illustrator

WARNING: spoilers ahead! Try out the prototype before your get spoilers!

Opening Sequence

the final project considers usability issues, by incorporating instructions at first, it clarifies the direction which the user is supposed to take

Surprise Sequence

This website features a surprise sequence, a reflection of the plotline which is mysterious and curious. The transition is very subtle, where the bottom caption changes from #/6 to ?/6, which indicates the beginning of the Surprise Sequence.

Hints

In the case that the player does not know what to do, the card will pop out using a red outline and a slight movement. Non-major cards will also get a black overlay to further emphasize the main component.

process

Ideation

The first phase was ideation, where I thought of creating a book, a card game, or i-spy game.

Lo-fi

This part started by sketching out the images in the each card and quickly prototyping the positions of the cards and how they would interact with the users.

Hi-fi

In the hi-fi stage, I converted the card images to SVG files and refined the prototyping, such as the transition periods and animations.

Critiques

Since this is a school project, we had weekly class sessions, in which she would check-in with out progress and provide feedback.

User Testing

In the user testing stage (before the final project was due), I asked a couple of classmates to try out the game and ask about their experience, if there were anything missing, or if something could be improved.

moving forward

Coding

This prototype is currently begin coded using HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

Accessibility

As this is a game, I hope to allow as many individuals to enjoy it as possible. I am going to conduct further user testing a to ensure the usability of this product. I am also looking to cross reference this product with the A11Y Project's checklist to ensure accessibility features are available.
