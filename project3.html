Savikalpa Academy

A website dedicated to cannabis education for both professionals and recreational customers. Created under the direction of White Elephant Agency.

Challenge

Savikalpa is a pre-existing company that has an established brand identity and its cannabis business. The client showed us the content that had been prepared for the site, which included about twenty documents, each three to four pages. The challenge with this site is to distinguish its Academy identity from its sister sites, while still maintaining its unique identity, and keeping the amount of information legible and easy to access.

Solution

The Savikalpa Academy website acts as a database for both professionals and non-professionals. The design system introduces new icons while maintaining the colour and typography system from its sister sites. The interface offers easy access to previous and next content, allowing users to easily navigate the information-heavy site.

Onboarding

This verifies the identity of users, which would allow Savikalpa Academy to be a more credible resource. These inputs may change upon publishing the website. In the case that users proceed to the website without an account, they will be prompted to sign in before access more content. This prompt exists in both the Education and Research page and Blog page.

Navigation

The design team utilized a master navigation bar and many table of contents for users to easily identify the topic they were looking for. There are three main navigation styles: one to navigate content within the page; one to navigate between pages under a larger section; and one to navigate across the website.

Resources

The Research and Education page and Blog page were both resources available for users. The Research and Education page was a main emphasis for the website, so we ensured that it was easy to understand and navigate. We also wanted to encourage users to explore the content, hence splitting different materials into two categories and providing them with relevant content if no results are shown. The Blog page is a collection of non-peer-reviewed articles, and it allowed users to interact with other users through comments and posts.

Process

Content

The client provided us with flushed-out content in great detail (including layout, hierarchy of text, and style guides), which allowed us to shorten the time we used to create low-fidelity wireframes.

Inspiration

Prior to creating wireframes, our team looked towards newspapers, academia sites, text-heavy websites to visualize how we could organize the text.

Design System

The client provided us with colour codes and the typeface they wanted to use, but still encouraged us to explore other options. We followed much of the design system as previously established by Savikalpa’s brand identity.
