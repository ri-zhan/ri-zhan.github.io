user research

We interviewed 9 young adults who were either in school or in a stressful field (??).

  • % possess a lack of self-reflection routine and do not have a convenient coping mechanism at hand which leads to a vicious cycle of having poor mental health.
  • % experience anxiety and fear when voicing their struggle mainly due to fear of judgement and a sense of uncomfortable vulnerability.
  • % do not journal or self-reflect consistently due to various reasons, but stated that they would like to do these things consistently or on a regular basis as they see the benefit in doing so.
  • % said that they are unaware of available resources, or are aware but do not have an easy way to access said resources.
  • % struggle with documenting and pinpointing a pattern in their emotions, often caused by a lack of safe space, time, or motivation.

expert advice

As mental health is a sensitive topic, we also interviewed a psychologist, who provided us with expertise on the topic that guided our design decisions.

  • Individuals may fixate on negative thoughts during an emotion-heavy process, especially self-reflection.
  • Encourage mindfulness and provide positive reinforcement

personas

Resources

The Research and Education page and Blog page were both resources available for users. The Research and Education page was a main emphasis for the website, so we ensured that it was easy to understand and navigate. We also wanted to encourage users to explore the content, hence splitting different materials into two categories and providing them with relevant content if no results are shown. The Blog page is a collection of non-peer-reviewed articles, and it allowed users to interact with other users through comments and posts.

process

Content

The client provided us with flushed-out content in great detail (including layout, hierarchy of text, and style guides), which allowed us to shorten the time we used to create low-fidelity wireframes.

Inspiration

Prior to creating wireframes, our team looked towards newspapers, academia sites, text-heavy websites to visualize how we could organize the text.

Design System

The client provided us with colour codes and the typeface they wanted to use, but still encouraged us to explore other options. We followed much of the design system as previously established by Savikalpa’s brand identity.
