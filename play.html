This book creates an experience of taking notice of often mundane, unseen, unintentional, yet impactful objects. Each booklet slowly brings the object closer and closer, until it’s the only subject present. It is a slow-paced, oversized book, demanding your attention due to its substantial presence, yet compelling you to see the details of the photographs as you move through. The booklets are not meant to be presented in a particular order, allowing readers to form their own interpretations. The film photographs are intentional and curated, ironic to the subject of this book. Notice the beauty of ordinary spaces and objects.

F.E.R.N

F.E.R.N stands for Floral Empathetic Rehabilitation Network. It is a multi-stage robotic plant that assists remote stroke rehabilitation patients through their rehabilitation program. By gamifying motor movements, it enhances the mood, emphasizes autonomy, and alleviates patients’ feelings of social redundancy. Each unit provides individualized care and exercises specifically programmed using the patient’s performance metrics, accessible in the health-companion dashboard. Using the UN SDGs as a guiding standard for social good, this product aims to "ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all ages", "reduce inequality within and among countries", and "ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns."
